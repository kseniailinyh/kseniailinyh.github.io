Mischief

One of my superpowers is mischief: I love using funny and entertaining tricks in my prototypes and processes. I've been working on B2B products for most of my career, so there often were not very exciting tasks. My funny tricks help me to bring joy into my everyday work.

Design Wars

In 2015, I was working on a product without a design system. There were 3 product designers including me and each time we designed a new version of the same components, such as inputs, buttons, combo boxes, etc. Once, I realized that there was a lot of inconsistency in my product, and I wanted to fix it.

I started to build a design system and involved other product designers in it. But design system is not only a Sketch library (Figma hasn't existed yet), it is also a developer's tools. So we needed help from our front-end engineers.

I was sure that almost every engineer loves Star Wars. And I came up with an idea to use one of the most popular opening crawl. I made a video for my colleagues to tell them what we want. With this video, I wanted to show them how important our task is. Front-end engineers were exciting and agreed to help. They chose the light side of the Force.

Lumpy Space Princess

Once, I've been working on a task with form validation. There were a lot of constraints so I needed to design more than 20 tooltips and write error messages. It was not a very exciting task.

But Lumpy Space Princess from the TV show "Adventure Time" helped me. On every screen, she told me about a constraint and how to turn it into a concise error message.

So she helped to diversify a boring task not only for me but also for an engineer who worked with me.

Comic

Once, I had the task of switching on a directory of addresses in my product. In Russia, it is a big directory with multiple levels of hierarchy. There are a lot of connectedness and relationships between levels. And all these relations must be shown in the interface. So I prepared a detailed description - it was a document with more than 10 pages.

Later I discussed the task with a backend developer. He said that he doesn't read task descriptions because they are boring. I didn't get was he serious or not, but said "Challenge accepted!".

So I used xkcd characters and drew a comic with him and me. In the comic, I tell him about the task with pictures. The developer was delighted and did everything right.

Storytelling works! It is more exciting to read a comic about a developer and designer than a drily technical description.
